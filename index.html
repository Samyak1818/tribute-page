Bill Gates

1955-Present

"Success is a lousy teacher. It seduces smart people into thinking they can't lose. It's fine to celebrate success but it is more important to heed the lessons of failure." -Bill Gates

Timeline

1955 Born in Seattle in a rich Business Family

1968 Enrolled in Lakeside School. Started Programming in the same Year

1970 Selected to write a payroll program in Cobol

1972 Gates teamed up with Allen to form a venture called Traf-O-Data, to make traffic counters based on the Intel 8008 processor.

1973 Graduated from High School.He enrolled at Harvard College later the same year. As a college student he spent a lot of time on the computers though he was not much interested in studying other subjects. His friend Allen suggested that Bill drop out of college to start a business.

1975 Collaborated with Paul Allen to found Microsoft (initially called Micro-Soft)

1980 The duo was approached by International Business Machine (IBM) with a proposal that Microsoft write the BASIC interpreter for IBM’s upcoming personal computer, the IBM PC. Microsoft created the PC DOS operating system which they delivered to IBM in exchange for a one-time fee of $50,000.

1985 Introduced an operating environment named Windows

1989 Introduced Microsoft Office.In the same year Gates met Melinda French working at Microsoft.

1990 Gates focused Microsoft on the development of consumer and enterprise software solutions for the Internet.

1994 He acquired the Codex Leicester—a collection of writings by Leonardo da Vinci—at an auction for $30.8 million And in the same year Gates married to Melinda French.

2000 Gates stepped down as CEO of Microsoft but retained his position as Chairman

2002 Gates and Melinda received Jefferson Award for Greatest Public Service Benefiting the Disadvantaged.

2010 Gates received the Bower Award for Business Leadership from The Franklin Institute in 2010 in recognition of his achievements at Microsoft and his philanthropic work.

2013 Gates became the world's highest-earning billionaire.

2014 He stepped down as Chairman of Microsoft

2015 Bill and Melinda Gates jointly received India's third highest civilian honor Padma Bhushan for their foundation's philanthropic activities in India.
